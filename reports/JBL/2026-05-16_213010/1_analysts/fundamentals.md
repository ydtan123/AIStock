Jabil Inc. is a global manufacturing solutions and services provider. The company operates as a contract manufacturer across diverse end markets including automotive, healthcare, packaging, consumer electronics, cloud/data center infrastructure, and industrial sectors. Jabil's business model is asset-intensive, characterized by high revenue turnover, thin margins, and significant customer concentration in the electronics supply chain.

1. **Significant Acquisition:** Goodwill surged from $841M to $1.229B (+$388M) and intangibles from $273M to $648M (+$375M) in two quarters. This clearly indicates a major acquisition was completed in H1 FY2026. The acquired entity's contribution to the revenue acceleration is likely material.

4. **Working Capital:** Current assets of $15.01B vs. current liabilities of $14.81B yields a razor-thin current ratio of **1.01x**. Working capital is only ~$198M on a $20B+ asset base. This is characteristic of the contract manufacturing model where payables and receivables are tightly matched, but it leaves little margin for error.

5. **Aggressive Share Buybacks Continue:** Shares outstanding declined from 109.2M (FY2025) to 107.6M (Feb 2026), a 1.5% reduction in just two quarters. $300M was spent on buybacks in each of Q1 and Q2 FY2026.

## 9. Risk Factors & Concerns

1. **Excessive Leverage:** Debt-to-equity of 3.27x and negative tangible book value create meaningful financial risk. In an economic downturn, debt service could become challenging.

2. **Thin Margins:** Net margins of 2.0–2.7% leave very little room for error. A modest cost overrun or pricing pressure could quickly erase profitability.

3. **Acquisition Integration Risk:** The recent acquisition (evidenced by goodwill/intangibles jump) needs to be successfully integrated. Acquisition-related charges and restructuring costs may pressure near-term earnings.

4. **Customer Concentration:** As a contract manufacturer, Jabil likely has significant customer concentration, though specific data is not disclosed in these financials.

5. **Cyclical Exposure:** Electronic components and manufacturing are cyclical industries. The current strong revenue growth may reflect an upcycle.

6. **Working Capital Tightness:** Current ratio of 1.01x provides essentially no liquidity buffer.

## 12. Conclusion

Jabil Inc. presents a **high-risk, high-reward profile**. The company is executing well operationally — delivering strong revenue growth, expanding gross margins, and generating robust free cash flow. The PEG ratio below 1.0 and aggressive share buybacks suggest the stock may offer value. However, the balance sheet has become increasingly leveraged, tangible equity is negative, and margins remain paper-thin. The recent acquisition, while apparently accretive to revenue, has added significant debt and integration complexity.

The stock's momentum is clearly positive (trading well above both 50-day and 200-day moving averages), but it already trades above the consensus analyst target. The decision to **BUY, HOLD, or SELL** ultimately depends on one's risk tolerance: the growth story is compelling, but the financial risk is non-trivial.

> **FINAL TRANSACTION PROPOSAL: **HOLD**** — While Jabil's growth metrics are impressive and the PEG ratio is attractive, the combination of extreme financial leverage (3.27x D/E), negative tangible book value, thin liquidity (1.01x current ratio), and the stock trading above analyst consensus targets warrants caution. Existing shareholders should maintain positions to capture ongoing buyback benefits, but the risk/reward does not clearly favor new entry at current levels pending further clarity on acquisition integration and deleveraging progress.
