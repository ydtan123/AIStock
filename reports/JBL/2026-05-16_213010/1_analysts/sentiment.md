## I. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Jabil Inc. experienced a week of high drama — surging to new all-time highs early in the period before a sharp ~7% correction from peak to trough. The stock hit a **52-week high of $372.37** intraweek, powered by AI infrastructure euphoria, then reversed sharply on Friday, May 15, closing at **$339.82** (down 4.1% on the day). Morgan conference and the June 16 Q3 earnings report.

### A. Ex-Dividend (May 15)

The $0.08 quarterly dividend (payable June 2) is small — a yield of barely 0.1% — but its mechanical effect on May 15 was amplified by existing selling pressure. The ex-dividend drop of ~$0.08 was dwarfed by the actual $14+ decline, confirming that **valuation concerns, not the dividend, were the real driver.**

### B. J.P. Morgan Global TMT Conference (May 19)

Management's fireside chat at this prestigious conference is now the **nearest-term catalyst.** Investors and traders alike will be watching for:
- Updates on the **Intelligent Infrastructure** segment, particularly AI data center demand trends
- Any revision to margin guidance for the infrastructure business
- Color on the $13.1 billion AI-related revenue target for FY2026
- Signals about the **Sivers Semiconductors optical transceiver partnership** ramp

### C. AI Revenue Trajectory

Jabil has guided to **$13.1 billion in AI-related sales** for FY2026, growing at ~34% YoY. The $500M U.S. facility expansion dedicated to AI data center infrastructure underscores the seriousness of this pivot. This is the core bull case — and the primary reason institutional money continues to flow in.

### D. Upcoming Earnings (Q3 FY2026, ~June 16)

Consensus estimates:
- **EPS:** $3.08 (+20.78% YoY)
- **Revenue:** $8.53 billion
- **Full-year FY2026 EPS:** $11.30–$12.30

This is the real "make or break" event. If Jabil delivers on these numbers and raises guidance, the valuation premium may look justified. A miss, however, would be severely punished given the elevated multiples.

## IX. TRADING & INVESTMENT INSIGHTS

### For Short-Term Traders

1. **The May 19 J.P. Morgan Conference is a binary catalyst.** Positive commentary on AI demand and margin expansion could quickly reverse the Friday selloff. Cautious or "inline" commentary could extend the correction toward the $319 support level.

2. **The $332–$340 zone is a battleground.** This range corresponds to the post-correction consolidation area. A hold above $332 would suggest the bull trend remains intact; a break below opens the door to $319 and potentially $265.

3. **Watch the RSI.** At 54.30, JBL is now neutral — neither overbought nor oversold. This means the stock has room to move in either direction without being technically "extended."

4. **Volume on down days matters.** The May 15 drop should be analyzed for whether it was high-volume distribution (bearish) or normal ex-dividend/profit-taking (less concerning).

### For Medium-Term Investors

1. **The insider selling pattern is the single biggest concern.** While the institutional inflows are reassuring, the asymmetry between insider sales and zero insider buying at multi-year highs warrants caution. This doesn't mean the stock can't go higher — but it does mean corporate leadership isn't betting their own money on it.

2. **Q3 earnings (~June 16) is the real litmus test.** Jabil needs to deliver at least $3.08 EPS on $8.53B revenue AND provide constructive Q4/2027 guidance to justify the current multiple. A "beat and raise" could propel the stock through $370 resistance; a miss could trigger a re-rating toward the analyst consensus target of ~$290.

3. **Position sizing is critical.** Given the ~97% GF Value overvaluation flag and the DCF intrinsic value of ~$353, new long entries at $340+ carry limited margin of safety. Accumulating on pullbacks toward $319–$332 offers better risk/reward.

4. **The "picks and shovels" thesis is durable.** Regardless of which AI company wins the software war, the physical infrastructure — servers, cooling, optical interconnects — must be built. Jabil is well-positioned in this layer of the stack.

## XI. BOTTOM LINE

Jabil Inc. enters the week of May 18 at a critical juncture. The AI infrastructure thesis is real, the institutional money is buying, and the company's pivot toward higher-margin segments is bearing fruit. However, the stock's extraordinary run (~100% in 12 months, ~50% YTD) has stretched valuations to levels that leave virtually no room for error. Social media sentiment, while net constructive, has shifted from "can't miss" to "show me" — a healthy evolution that reduces downside risk from euphoric capitulation but also signals that the easy money has been made.

The May 19 J.P. Morgan fireside chat is the immediate catalyst to watch. Traders should position with discipline; investors should consider phased accumulation on pullbacks. Either way, JBL is no longer a stealth play — the market is fully aware of the AI infrastructure story, and the burden of proof now rests squarely on management's ability to deliver.
