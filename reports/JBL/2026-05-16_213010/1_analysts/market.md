- **Lower Band Target:** The lower band sits at $321.98. In mean-reverting Bollinger Band logic, once the middle band is broken, the lower band becomes a natural target. This implies another ~5.3% of potential downside.

- **Band Expansion:** The bands have been widening since late April (width expanding from ~$30 to ~$44.50), reflecting surging volatility. Expanding bands after a long uptrend can signal a transition from trending to a more volatile, choppy, or corrective phase.

### 8. Synthesis & Trade Implications

**The Bull Case (Long-Term):**
- Price remains comfortably above both the 50 SMA and 200 SMA in a textbook uptrend.
- The 50/200 SMA golden cross and widening spread confirm the trend's strength.
- The current pullback could simply be a healthy correction within a secular bull move.

**The Bear Case (Short-to-Medium Term):**
- MACD bearish divergence is the single most concerning signal — it preceded the peak.
- Price has broken below the 10 EMA and Bollinger middle band simultaneously.
- RSI has collapsed from overbought to neutral with no sign of stabilization.
- Heavy volume distribution at the highs.
- The gap between price and the 50 SMA (~12.9%) is historically wide, inviting mean reversion.

**Overall Assessment:**
The weight of evidence favors a **HOLD** for existing long positions, with a strong bias toward caution. Adding new longs at current levels carries elevated risk until either (a) price reclaims the 10 EMA and Bollinger middle band (~$345–$349), or (b) price finds clear support and forms a base (potentially at the lower Bollinger band ~$322 or the 50 SMA ~$301). Aggressive traders might consider short-term bearish plays with a target near the lower Bollinger band, but the long-term uptrend argues against large short positions without further confirmation.
