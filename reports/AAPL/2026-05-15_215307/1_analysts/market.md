# AAPL Technical Analysis Report — May 15, 2026

## 1. Executive Summary

AAPL is in the midst of a **powerful, accelerating bull run** that has carried the stock from approximately $253 (April 7 low) to near $300 in just over five weeks — a gain of roughly **18.5%**. The technical picture is overwhelmingly bullish, with all three key moving averages stacked in perfect bullish alignment. However, multiple indicators are now flashing **overbought/extended warnings** that demand trader attention. The rally is mature, and while the trend remains firmly up, the probability of a consolidation or sharp pullback is rising.

This is the "all clear" for long-term holders — no death cross risk whatsoever. The 200 SMA hasn't been tested since the August 2025 selloff, when AAPL dipped to ~$202.

- **50 SMA ($265.17)**: Rising at a healthy clip of ~$4.60/month. Crucially, the 50 SMA crossed **above** the 200 SMA around April 15-16, 2026 — a classic **Golden Cross**. This crossover occurred with the 50 SMA at ~$260.55 vs. the 200 SMA at ~$250.98, and the gap has since widened to nearly $7. This is a powerful medium-term bullish confirmation.

- **10 EMA ($289.68)**: Rising at a blistering pace — ~$31 in 30 days (~12% monthly rate). The 10 EMA is acting as dynamic support; the last two touches (May 7 at $287.44 and May 11 at $292.68) both held and led to sharp bounces. The 10 EMA's steep slope is characteristic of a momentum-driven rally, not a slow grind.

> **Key Insight**: The widening gap between price and the 50 SMA ($33 gap) is a double-edged sword. It confirms strong momentum but also signals that the stock is extended far beyond its mean. Historically, gaps this wide tend to close — either through price consolidation (trading sideways while the MA catches up) or a corrective pullback.

The MACD is in uncharted territory at **9.21** — this is an extraordinarily high reading that reflects the ferocity of the recent rally. The MACD crossed above its signal line around April 16-17, and the bullish crossover has been unbroken for a full month.

**Critical observation — Histogram inflection**: The MACD histogram peaked at **2.07 on May 13** and ticked down to **1.91 on May 14**. This is the first contraction after a sustained expansion phase. While one day does not make a trend, histogram peaks often precede price peaks by 2-5 sessions. Traders should watch closely: if the histogram continues to contract over the next 2-3 days, it would constitute a meaningful bearish divergence signal — momentum weakening while price holds near highs.

The histogram also shows a pattern of **lower histogram peaks** relative to the rapid price ascent:
- April 22 peak: 1.84 (price ~$273)
- April 24 peak: 1.53 (price ~$271)
- May 1 peak: 0.91 (price ~$280)
- May 8 peak: 1.99 (price ~$293)
- May 13 peak: 2.07 (price ~$299)

While the most recent histogram peaks are at fresh highs, the acceleration from May 8-13 was sharp, and the May 14 dip may mark exhaustion.

## 4. Scenario Analysis

### Scenario A — Continuation (35% probability)
Price breaks above $300 with conviction, RSI pushes toward 80, MACD histogram resumes expansion. The 10 EMA at $290 acts as support. Target: $310-315. Invalidated if price closes below the 10 EMA ($289.68).

### Scenario B — Consolidation (45% probability)

### Scenario C — Pullback/Correction (20% probability)
RSI overbought exhaustion triggers a 5-8% correction. First support: 10 EMA at $289.68. Second support: May 7 gap fill area around $285-287. Stronger support: 50 SMA at $265.17 (a 11% decline from current levels — painful but trend-preserving). A close below the 50 SMA would be the first meaningful trend-damage signal.
