# FTI (TechnipFMC plc) — Comprehensive Technical Analysis

**Analysis Date: May 16, 2026 | Latest Close: $71.28 (May 15, 2026)**

---

## Executive Summary

FTI is in a **robust long-term uptrend** but is currently undergoing a **meaningful short-term correction**. The stock has pulled back approximately **6.9%** from its recent peak of $76.56 (May 5, 2026) and is now testing critical support at the 50-day SMA ($70.56). Momentum indicators have turned decisively bearish in the near term, with a confirmed MACD bearish crossover and RSI retreating from overbought territory into neutral-bearish levels. However, the broader structural uptrend remains firmly intact, with the 50 SMA towering nearly $19 above the 200 SMA. The coming sessions will be pivotal — a hold above the 50 SMA could present a tactical buying opportunity, while a breakdown would signal a deeper correction.

## 1. Trend Analysis — Moving Averages

### 50-Day SMA ($70.56) — Medium-Term Trend
The 50 SMA has been in a persistent, smooth uptrend throughout the entire observation window, rising from approximately $58.68 (March 17) to $70.56 (May 15). This represents a gain of ~20% in just two months for the average itself, underscoring the strength of the intermediate-term advance. Critically, the 50 SMA has served as **reliable dynamic support** — every dip toward it over the past several months has been bought aggressively.

As of May 15, price closed at $71.28, a mere **$0.72 above the 50 SMA**. This is the closest the stock has been to its 50 SMA since the uptrend accelerated in late March. The proximity to this level makes it the single most important technical threshold in play right now.

### 200-Day SMA ($51.53) — Long-Term Trend
The 200 SMA stands at $51.53 and has risen steadily from $43.59 in mid-March. The slope is healthy and accelerating. The price premium over the 200 SMA is enormous — approximately **38.3%**, which confirms an exceptionally strong secular bull market in FTI. The 200 SMA is far too distant to act as actionable support, but its steep upward trajectory confirms that the big-picture trend is not in question.

### Golden Cross / Trend Structure
The 50 SMA crossed above the 200 SMA well before our observation window (the "golden cross"), and the spread has only widened — from ~15 points in March to ~19 points in May. This is textbook bullish trend structure. Importantly, there is **zero evidence** of a pending "death cross" or even convergence between the two averages. The long-term trend remains unequivocally bullish.

## 2. Momentum Analysis — MACD

### MACD Line: 0.3145 | Signal Line: 0.8187
The MACD complex tells a story of **rapidly deteriorating momentum**. Here's the chronology:

- **Mid-March to Mid-April**: MACD rose strongly, peaking at **2.6872 on April 13**, while the signal line lagged, creating a wide positive histogram. This coincided with the stock's powerful rally from ~$64 to $75+.

- **Bearish Crossover (~April 16–17)**: The MACD line sliced below the signal line (MACD: 2.2545 vs Signal: 2.3816 on April 16), triggering a classic sell signal. This crossover occurred while price was still near its highs — a warning that the rally was losing internal momentum even as price held up.

- **Post-Crossover Acceleration**: Since the crossover, the MACD line has collapsed from 2.25 to 0.31 — a **decline of 86% in one month**. The signal line has fallen more slowly (from 2.38 to 0.82), which means the histogram (MACD − Signal) is now deeply negative at **−0.504**.

- **Zero Line Risk**: The MACD line at 0.31 is dangerously close to crossing below the zero line. A sub-zero MACD reading would confirm a shift from bullish to bearish momentum on the intermediate timeframe and could accelerate selling pressure.

## 4. Volatility & Price Structure — Bollinger Bands

### Bollinger Middle (20 SMA): $73.35 | Upper Band: $77.26 | Lower Band: $69.44

The Bollinger Bands provide critical context for the current price action:

- **Band Width**: The bands span from $69.44 to $77.26, a width of **$7.82 (~10.7% of price)**. This is elevated and reflects the heightened volatility of recent weeks.

- **Price Relative to Bands**: Price at $71.28 sits **below the middle band ($73.35)** — a definitively bearish posture. After riding the upper band during the late-April/early-May surge (touching $76.56 on May 5, just shy of the then-upper-band at $77.27), the stock has now migrated to the lower half of the band envelope. This is a classic "walk down" from the upper to the lower band, which often signals a trend exhaustion or reversal pattern.

- **Lower Band Target**: The lower band at $69.44 is only **$1.84 below current price**. If the correction continues, this level becomes a natural magnet and potential support zone. Notably, the May 8 low of $70.13 came within $0.69 of the then-lower-band. A retest or break of the lower band could trigger a more decisive move.

- **Band Slope**: Both the middle and lower bands have flattened in the past two weeks after rising steeply through March and April. The flattening middle band is itself a sign that the uptrend is consolidating or stalling.

- **The "Band Ride" That Ended**: From April 24 to May 5, price hugged or exceeded the upper Bollinger Band — a classic "band ride" in strong trends. The failure to sustain that ride and the subsequent drop below the middle band is a significant technical deterioration.

## 6. Synthesis: The Big Picture

FTI is at a **critical technical inflection point**. Here is the bull vs. bear case:

### Bull Case (Buy-the-Dip):
- The 50 SMA at $70.56 is historically strong support. Every prior tag has held.
- The 200 SMA is miles below at $51.53 — the long-term trend is not in question.
- RSI at 46.52 is cooling toward levels where prior corrections ended.
- The lower Bollinger Band ($69.44) provides a nearby support floor.
- If the 50 SMA holds, this correction is a healthy reset within a secular bull market, offering an attractive risk/reward entry near $70–71.

### Bear Case (Correction Deepens):
- MACD is crashing toward zero and the histogram is deeply negative — momentum is still deteriorating.
- RSI has no bullish divergence; the correction may not be done.
- Price is below the Bollinger middle and walking down toward the lower band.
- The failed bounce on May 11–12 indicates sellers are in control short-term.
- A break below the 50 SMA could trigger a swift move to the $67–68 area (prior support from late March/early April), representing an additional 5–6% downside.

### Most Likely Scenario:
The weight of evidence suggests the correction has **more work to do** before a durable low is established. The 50 SMA is likely to be tested ($70.56), and a brief undercut toward the lower Bollinger Band ($69.44) is plausible. However, given the strength of the underlying trend, this zone ($69.50–$70.50) represents a high-probability support area where buyers would be expected to defend. A close back above the Bollinger middle ($73.35) would be the first signal that the correction has run its course and the uptrend is resuming.
