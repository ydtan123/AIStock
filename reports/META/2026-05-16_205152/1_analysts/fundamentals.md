## 1. COMPANY OVERVIEW

Meta Platforms, Inc. (formerly Facebook, Inc.) is a global technology company headquartered in Menlo Park, California. The company builds products that enable people to connect and share through mobile devices, PCs, virtual reality headsets, wearables, and home devices. Its core properties include Facebook, Instagram, Messenger, WhatsApp, and its Reality Labs division focused on augmented/virtual reality and the metaverse. The company has a December fiscal year-end, with the latest reported quarter ending March 31, 2026.

## 9. RISK FACTORS & CONCERNS

1. **Massive CapEx Cycle:** $69.7B in FY2025 and accelerating. If AI monetization does not materialize as expected, these investments could pressure returns. However, the advertising business alone generates enough cash to fund this.

2. **Regulatory & Tax Risk:** The Q3 2025 one-time tax charge of ~$18.95B highlights exposure to international tax policy changes and regulatory fines (EU, U.S. antitrust).

3. **Concentrated Revenue:** Advertising remains the dominant revenue source. Reality Labs generated negligible revenue relative to its investment, though AI-driven ad improvements are already showing in results.

4. **SBC Dilution:** $20.4B in annual stock-based compensation is meaningful, though buybacks have fully offset dilution thus far.

5. **Interest Expense Rising:** Net interest income flipped from positive $2.3B (FY2025) to only $182M in Q1 2026 as debt increased and cash deployed. Interest expense of $562M in Q1 2026 annualizes to ~$2.25B.
