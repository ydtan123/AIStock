### 1.1 The "Tariff Truce" — A Defining Event

The week was dominated by the **U.S.-China 90-day tariff pause** announced on May 12, 2025. The U.S. agreed to cap tariffs on Chinese goods at 30%, while China set retaliatory tariffs at 10%. This triggered a **relief rally in global equities**, particularly in logistics, tech, and consumer discretionary sectors. However, the relief is inherently **temporary** — a 90-day window rather than a structural resolution — creating a "front-loading" frenzy where Chinese-to-U.S. container bookings surged 300% as importers rushed to move goods.

- **UN Global Growth Outlook:** Slashed to 2.4% from 2.9%.
- **IMF:** U.S. 2025 growth forecast cut from 2.7% to 1.8%.

This environment is **negative for broad equity valuations** but **supportive for gold and defensive assets**. For AEM (semiconductor equipment), it creates a mixed picture: AI-driven demand is relatively insulated, but broader industrial/consumer chip demand could soften.

### 1.4 Fiscal Policy — The "Bond Vigilante" Narrative

The **House Ways and Means Committee** passed a $5 trillion tax cut bill alongside $1.5 trillion in spending cuts (Medicaid/green energy). This fiscal expansion, combined with sticky inflation, drove the **U.S. 10-year Treasury yield to a 3-month high of 4.54%**. Higher risk-free rates pressure equity valuations across the board, including growth/tech names like AEM.

The U.S. federal deficit is now projected to rise by $3.8 trillion through 2034, adding further upward pressure on long-end yields.
