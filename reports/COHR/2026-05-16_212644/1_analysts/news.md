# COHR (Coherent Corp.) — Weekly Intelligence Report
**Period: May 9–16, 2026 | Analyst: News Research Desk**

---

## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The week of May 9–16, 2026, was pivotal for Coherent Corp. (COHR). The stock digested its **Q3 FY2026 earnings blowout** (released May 6), triggering a cascade of analyst target raises, while the macro backdrop turned decisively more complex: a **hot CPI print (3.8% YoY)**, a **Fed leadership transition** to Kevin Warsh, and escalating **Iran-conflict energy shocks** combined to revive stagflation fears. For COHR specifically, the company is being re-rated as a **pure-play AI infrastructure linchpin**, cemented by its NVIDIA partnership and hyperscaler CapEx tailwinds. The key tension: stellar secular demand versus a macro environment that punishes high-multiple names.

## 2. COHR COMPANY-SPECIFIC ANALYSIS

### 2.1 Q3 FY2026 Earnings — The Numbers Behind the Surge

| Metric | Q3 FY2026 Actual | Consensus | YoY Change |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue | **$1.81B** | $1.78B | +21% |
| Non-GAAP EPS | **$1.41** | $1.36 | Beat by $0.05 |
| Non-GAAP Gross Margin | **39.6%** | — | Expanding |
| Datacenter & Comms Rev Share | **75%** | — | Up from 65% YoY |

**Q4 FY2026 Guidance (ending June 2026):**
- Revenue: **$1.91B–$2.05B** (midpoint ~$1.98B, above consensus ~$1.93B)
- Non-GAAP EPS: **$1.52–$1.72** (midpoint ~$1.62, above consensus ~$1.55)

The Datacenter & Communications segment is now the dominant revenue driver at 75% of the business, up from 65% a year ago. This reflects insatiable demand for **800G and 1.6T optical transceivers** powering AI cluster interconnects.

### 2.2 Analyst Actions — A Wave of Bullish Revisions

- **Stifel (May 11):** Price target raised to **$412** (from ~$275). Rating: **Buy**. Analyst Ruben Roy called COHR a "top pick," citing a 67% acceleration in U.S. hyperscaler CapEx and COHR's unassailable lead in **Indium Phosphide (InP) and EML lasers** for 800G/1.6T transceivers.

- **Bank of America (May 13):** Target raised to **$400** (from $365). Rating: **Neutral**. BofA acknowledged the massive AI TAM but flagged **valuation risk** (~45x forward P/E) as a reason to stay on the sidelines.

- **Northland Securities (May 16):** Raised **FY2027 EPS estimate to $6.87** (from $6.12) and **FY2026 EPS to $4.65**. This signals conviction that the growth cycle extends well beyond the current fiscal year — a multi-year compounder narrative.

- **Consensus:** "Moderate Buy" with median target ~$380–$385, but high-conviction bulls are in the $410–$455 range.

### 2.3 The NVIDIA Strategic Moat

The **$2 billion equity investment** from NVIDIA (announced prior but heavily analyzed this week) fundamentally alters COHR's risk profile:
- **Balance Sheet:** Cash position swells to ~$3 billion, de-risking debt and capacity expansion.
- **Supply Lock-In:** Multi-year agreement secures COHR as the preferred provider of **co-packaged optics (CPO)** and **1.6T transceivers** through the end of the decade.
- **Competitive Moat:** Raises barriers for competitors like Lumentum (LITE) and broadens the gap in InP manufacturing scale.

### 2.4 Capacity Expansion — Ahead of Schedule

Management confirmed during the week that COHR is tracking **one quarter ahead** of its internal roadmap to double **6-inch indium phosphide (InP) capacity** by end of calendar 2026. Additionally, the **Optical Circuit Switching (OCS)** total addressable market was revised upward to **>$4 billion** as production bottlenecks are resolved.

### 2.5 Insider Activity

Director **Howard H. Xia** exercised options and sold 2,000 shares on May 11 at prices between **$344 and $368**. Analysts characterized this as routine tax-planning / planned selling rather than a signal of deteriorating fundamentals.

### 2.6 Stock Price Action

Despite the beat-and-raise, COHR experienced a brief **~2.7% "sell-the-news" dip** early in the week, consistent with a market that had priced in significant upside. The stock recovered through the back half of the week as Stifel and Northland upgrades circulated.

## 3. MACROECONOMIC CONTEXT

### 3.1 U.S. Inflation Shock (CPI — May 12)

| Measure | April 2026 | Consensus | Prior Month |
|---|---|---|---|
| Headline CPI (YoY) | **3.8%** | 3.7% | 3.3% |
| Core CPI (YoY) | **2.8%** | — | — |
| Energy (YoY) | **+17.87%** | — | — |

This was the most consequential print of the week. The upside surprise, driven by energy costs (tied to the Iran conflict), triggered an **immediate hawkish repricing of the yield curve**. The "transitory" narrative is dead; inflation is proving sticky due to **supply-side shocks**, not just demand-pull.

**Implication for COHR:** As a high-growth, high-multiple name (~45x forward P/E), COHR is acutely sensitive to rising real yields. Higher discount rates compress the present value of future earnings. This macro headwind offsets some of the company-specific bullishness.

### 3.2 PPI Confirms Broadening Pressures (May 13)

The April PPI showed cost pressures **spreading beyond energy** into construction materials and manufacturing inputs. This is a leading indicator — upstream costs typically flow through to CPI with a lag. For COHR specifically, input cost inflation in semiconductor materials and specialty chemicals bears monitoring for **gross margin risk**, even as the company's pricing power in leading-edge optics provides some insulation.

### 3.3 Fed Leadership Transition (May 15)

Jerome Powell's term concluded, with **Kevin Warsh** expected to assume the Chair role. Warsh has historically been more skeptical of Fed overreach and more vocal about the need for accommodative policy — but the current 3.8% CPI environment may **force him into a hawkish posture** early in his tenure. Markets are pricing:

- **No rate cuts in H1 2026** (previously June was live).
- Increasing probability of a **hold through year-end**.
- Elevated volatility in USD and Treasury markets during the transition.

### 3.4 Consumer Demand Cracks (May 14)

Retail sales showed **signs of demand destruction**, with gasoline at a national average of **$4.54/gallon** acting as a regressive tax on consumers. Initial jobless claims ticked up to **200,000**. The combination of **rising inflation + softening consumption** has reignited **stagflation narratives** — a particularly toxic mix for equity markets broadly, and a headwind for the "risk-on" sentiment that fuels high-beta names like COHR.

### 3.5 Central Bank Divergence

| Central Bank | Action | Signal |
|---|---|---|
| **RBA (May 14)** | +25bps to 4.35% (3rd hike in 2026) | Hawkish; fighting broadening inflation |
| **Fed** | On hold; Warsh transition | Data-dependent hawkish tilt |
| **ECB** | Active hold | Cautious; watching energy spillovers |
| **BoJ** | Divided board | Normalization debate ongoing |

This divergence opens **FX carry trade opportunities** (long AUD vs. JPY/EUR), but also signals that global liquidity conditions are tightening unevenly — not a tailwind for growth equities.

### 3.6 China — Export Strength, Domestic Fragility (May 9)

China's April exports surged **+14.1% YoY**, well above expectations, as global manufacturers front-loaded component orders amid Iran-conflict supply-chain fears. However, domestic lending remained weak (~300B yuan), indicating China's internal recovery is fragile. For COHR: strong Chinese export data suggests robust global demand for electronics and photonics components, a modest tailwind.

### 3.7 Geopolitics — Iran Conflict & Oil Shock

The OMFIF analysis (May 15) argued that the Iran war has **permanently reshaped global supply chains**, prioritizing resilience over efficiency. The Fed's Financial Stability Report flagged **geopolitical risks and the oil shock** as the #1 threat to financial stability (cited by 75% of survey respondents). Prolonged conflict risks:

- Sustained upward pressure on energy prices.
- Increased margin requirements and volatility in energy derivatives.
- Structural shift toward **defense spending and domestic production**, keeping long-term real yields elevated.

For COHR, the geopolitical premium cuts both ways: (a) it drives demand for secure, domestic photonics supply chains (positive), but (b) it sustains the inflation/rates headwind (negative).

## 4. INTEGRATED RISK & OPPORTUNITY ASSESSMENT

### Bull Case for COHR
- **Secular AI Demand:** Hyperscaler CapEx accelerating 67%; COHR is the critical optical interconnect supplier.
- **NVIDIA Lock-In:** $2B investment + multi-year supply agreement creates a durable competitive moat.
- **Capacity Lead:** InP doubling ahead of schedule; OCS TAM expanding to $4B+.
- **Earnings Momentum:** FY2027 EPS estimates revised up to $6.87 — a multi-year compounder.
- **Backlog Visibility:** Orders extend into 2028, insulating against near-term macro softness.

### Bear Case / Risks
- **Valuation Compression:** ~45x forward P/E is vulnerable if 10Y yields push above 4.5% on sticky inflation.
- **Stagflation Macro:** Rising input costs + softening consumer = margin squeeze risk for the broader economy, potentially spilling into enterprise IT spending.
- **Execution Risk:** Doubling InP capacity is capital-intensive and operationally complex; any delays could disappoint stretched expectations.
- **Fed Policy Uncertainty:** Warsh transition introduces unpredictable policy risk; no rate cuts in 2026 would weigh heavily on growth multiples.
- **Energy Shock:** Sustained $4.50+/gal gasoline acts as a broad economic drag.

## 7. BOTTOM LINE

Coherent Corp. sits at the intersection of an extraordinary secular growth story (AI optical interconnects) and a deteriorating macro environment (sticky inflation, oil shock, Fed uncertainty). The company-specific fundamentals are among the strongest in the semiconductor/photonics space — NVIDIA's $2B anchor investment, hyperscaler CapEx acceleration, and ahead-of-schedule InP capacity expansion make a compelling case for sustained above-consensus growth. However, the ~45x forward multiple leaves no room for macro disappointment. A stagflationary environment that pushes the 10Y yield above 4.5% could trigger significant multiple compression even as earnings hold up.

**Trading View:** Long-term accumulation on dips driven by macro fear (not company-specific news) appears warranted. The case for an outright short is weak given the secular demand visibility. Near-term, the Warsh Fed transition and next CPI print (June) are the critical macro catalysts that will determine whether COHR's multiple can be sustained or must contract.
