## 1. Indicator Selection Rationale

For a stock like COHR exhibiting extreme volatility and a powerful trend, I selected **eight indicators** spanning four complementary domains:

| # | Indicator | Category | Rationale |
|---|-----------|----------|-----------|
| 1 | **close_10_ema** | Short-term MA | Captures immediate momentum shifts; essential for a stock making parabolic moves |
| 2 | **close_50_sma** | Medium-term MA | Serves as dynamic support and defines the intermediate trend |

| 5 | **macdh** | Momentum (histogram) | Early-warning system for momentum deceleration *before* MACD turns — critical for spotting divergences |
| 6 | **rsi** | Momentum oscillator | Flags overbought/oversold extremes and reveals hidden bearish/bullish divergences |
| 7 | **boll_ub** | Volatility (upper) | Identifies breakout zones and potential exhaustion when price tags or exceeds the band |
| 8 | **boll_lb** | Volatility (lower) | Maps downside risk and potential support floors during corrections |

These eight avoid redundancy: no StochRSI or multiple MACD signal variants. The three MAs span short, medium, and long timeframes without overlap; MACD + MACDH provide both trend-following and leading momentum signals; RSI adds a bounded oscillator; and the Bollinger Bands frame volatility context.
