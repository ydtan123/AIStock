**Recommendation: HOLD for AEM.SG**

**Reasoning**  
The comprehensive analysis from our team of analysts establishes a clear **Hold** rating, supported by the following key points:

- **Valuation risk is elevated.** At a forward P/E of 46× and a trailing PEG ratio of 28.15, the stock has priced in near‑perfect execution from the AI testing thesis. Any earnings miss – especially given high customer concentration – would trigger severe multiple compression.
- **Institutional activity signals caution.** Repeated substantial‑shareholder filings indicate systematic distribution, not routine rebalancing. While some dip‑buying may occur, the smart‑money trend is toward reducing exposure.
- **Customer diversification remains aspirational.** The promised new AI/HPC anchor customer has not been named, no contract has been signed, and no revenue has been booked. The market is discounting a future that has not yet materialised.
- **Macro headwinds are building.** Stagflationary conditions (U.S. GDP revised down, PCE inflation sticky at 3.8%) typically punish high‑multiple growth names. Liquidity tightening makes AEM’s valuation particularly vulnerable.
- **Risk‑reward is asymmetric to the downside.** For existing holders, the long‑term AI supercycle thesis still merits maintaining positions, but with strict risk management (e.g., a hard stop‑loss at S$9.50). For new buyers, initiating at current levels (S$10.40) offers no margin of safety; disciplined entry should target the **S$8.00–S$9.00** band.

The analysts’ plan identifies specific upgrade triggers – signed customer contracts, a pullback below 25× forward P/E, or evidence of renewed accumulation – which would support a Buy. Until those catalysts appear, **Hold** is the most analytically honest rating, balancing the structural growth story against the present overvaluation and macro risk.
