## I. MACROECONOMIC LANDSCAPE

### A. U.S. Economy: Stagflationary Signals Intensify

The most impactful macro development this week was the **simultaneous release of downward-revised Q1 GDP (1.6%) and elevated April PCE inflation (3.8%)** on May 28. This combination is the hallmark of a stagflationary environment:

- **GDP Revision:** The BEA cut Q1 growth from 2.0% to 1.6%, citing weaker inventory investment and a deceleration in consumer services spending. This signals that the consumer-driven recovery is losing momentum.
- **PCE Inflation:** At 3.8% YoY (headline) and 3.3% (core), inflation remains stubbornly above the Fed's 2% target. This gives the Fed—now transitioning to Kevin Warsh's leadership—limited room to cut rates.
- **Fed Leadership Transition:** The May 27 confirmation that Kevin Warsh will lead the June FOMC meeting, with Jerome Powell staying on as a Governor, creates unusual uncertainty. Markets are pricing in a more hawkish/neutral bias under Warsh, which has increased fixed-income volatility.

**Trading Implication:** A "higher for longer" rate environment is now more entrenched. Growth stocks with high valuations (like CRDO) face a double bind—strong AI demand is a tailwind, but lofty P/E ratios are vulnerable to rate-related derating.

### B. Geopolitical: U.S.-Iran Ceasefire Extension Boosts Risk Appetite

The **preliminary 60-day ceasefire extension between the U.S. and Iran** (reported May 29) was a major positive catalyst this week:

- **Oil Prices:** Crude oil declined sharply as fears of a Strait of Hormuz disruption receded, lowering input costs for the broader economy.
- **Equity Markets:** The S&P 500 hit record highs on the news, with risk-on sentiment reviving growth and tech stocks.
- **Energy Sector:** Bearish for energy stocks; bullish for sectors that benefit from lower energy costs (transportation, industrials, data centers).

### C. Europe: Inflation Accelerates, ECB Hawkishness Rising

Flash CPI data for May showed worrying acceleration:
- **France:** 2.8% (above estimates)
- **Spain:** 3.6% (above estimates)

Surging energy costs prior to the ceasefire news were the main culprit. This increases the probability of an ECB rate hike in June, supporting the EUR/USD but raising borrowing costs for European companies.

### D. China: Stagnation Continues

- **Manufacturing PMI:** 50.0 (flat, at the expansion/contraction boundary)
- **Non-Manufacturing PMI:** 50.1 (barely expansion)

China's industrial sector remains stagnant, which is bearish for commodities like copper and iron ore. This has indirect implications for tech hardware companies reliant on Asian supply chains.

### E. Global Sentiment: WEF Chief Economists Warn of Weakening

89% of chief economists expect global growth to weaken over the next year, citing the Middle East conflict and supply chain disruptions. This suggests defensive positioning may be prudent long-term, though the near-term ceasefire relief has lifted risk appetite.

- **Technology Vertical Integration:** Credo can now design and produce optical transceivers and ZeroFlap™ optics internally, rather than relying on third-party suppliers.

3. **Ceasefire Tailwind:** Lower oil prices and improved risk appetite benefit high-beta growth stocks.

### The Bear Case for CRDO:
1. **Stagflation Macro Headwind:** With GDP at 1.6% and PCE at 3.8%, the macro environment is hostile to high-multiple stocks. If the Fed stays hawkish under Warsh, growth stocks face valuation compression.
2. **Valuation Extreme:** At 120–130x earnings, CRDO is pricing in perfection. Any guidance miss (especially on gross margins, projected at 64–66%) could trigger a sharp selloff.
3. **Insider Selling:** $50M+ in insider sales over 90 days is a red flag.
4. **Execution Risk:** The DustPhotonics integration is complex; $750M in cash is a big bet.
