## 🏛️ 1. U.S. Macroeconomic Landscape

### GDP Revision: Cooling Growth

The BEA's second estimate of Q1 2026 GDP was revised down sharply to **1.6% annualized** (from 2.0% initial). The deceleration was driven by:
- Weaker inventory investment
- Slowing consumer spending on services
- Persistent drag from net exports

**Trading Implication:** This is a clear signal of economic softening. For high-growth names like CRDO (trading at ~120-130x P/E), a slowing macro raises the bar for earnings beats to justify multiples. However, if growth slows enough to eventually force Fed easing, it could be a tailwind for duration-sensitive growth stocks.

### PCE Inflation: Sticky at 3.8%

The April PCE reading of **3.8% YoY** (Core PCE: 3.3%) confirms inflation remains entrenched above the Fed's 2% target. This creates a **policy dilemma** — the economy is slowing, but prices aren't cooperating.

**Key Tension:** The new Fed leadership under Kevin Warsh (taking over at the June FOMC) faces a "stagflation-lite" scenario. Markets are pricing a more hawkish tilt from Warsh compared to Powell, but the GDP miss may moderate that view.

### U.S.-Iran Ceasefire Extension (May 29)

The preliminary **60-day ceasefire** between the U.S. and Iran was the most impactful geopolitical event of the week:
- Triggered a **sharp drop in crude oil** prices
- Drove the S&P 500 to **record highs**
- Reduced the "war risk premium" baked into energy and defense stocks

**Trading Implication:** Lower energy costs directly benefit the operating margins of hyperscalers and data center operators — CRDO's primary customer base. This is a **positive indirect tailwind** for CRDO, as lower opex budgets for cloud/AI customers could sustain or increase CapEx allocation to networking infrastructure.

## 🌍 2. European & Global Developments

### Eurozone Inflation Surprise

French CPI hit **2.8%** and Spanish CPI hit **3.6%** — both above estimates. This raises the probability of the ECB pursuing further rate hikes in June, strengthening the EUR/USD. A stronger EUR could impact U.S. multinational earnings but has limited direct effect on CRDO (a U.S.-domiciled semiconductor play with global hyperscaler customers).

### China PMI: Stagnant at 50.0

China's official Manufacturing PMI came in at exactly **50.0**, signaling no expansion. The Non-Manufacturing PMI of 50.1 confirms a fragile recovery. For CRDO, China exposure is less direct (its primary customers are U.S. hyperscalers), but a stagnant China could dampen global semiconductor demand sentiment broadly.

### WEF Chief Economists' Outlook

89% of chief economists expect global growth to weaken. This is a contrarian signal against the recent equity rally. For CRDO, the key question is whether **AI infrastructure spending** is recession-proof or will be pulled back if enterprise customers tighten budgets.
