# Macro & TSLA Weekly News Report: May 24 – May 31, 2026

## Executive Summary

The final week of May 2026 was a period of **divergent signals** for traders. On the macro front, **stagflationary undertones** emerged in the U.S. (GDP revised down to 1.6% while PCE inflation stayed sticky at 3.8%), while **geopolitical de-escalation** (U.S.-Iran ceasefire extension) provided a relief rally for equities. For Tesla (TSLA), the stock took a **technical breather after a 6-day winning streak**, closing the week at **$435.79**, still up ~6% for the week and ~17% for the month. The narrative was dominated by **massive capex escalation ($25B+)** for AI infrastructure and growing speculation around a **SpaceX synergy/merger "Endgame."**

## 1. MACROECONOMIC LANDSCAPE – Key Developments

### A. U.S. – Stagflationary Signals
- **Q1 2026 GDP Revised Down to 1.6%** (from 2.0% initial estimate) — weaker inventory investment and softening consumer spending on services.
- **April PCE Inflation at 3.8% YoY** (Core PCE 3.3%) — well above the Fed's 2% target, limiting scope for rate cuts.
- **Result:** A "growth scare" meets "sticky inflation." The probability of a June rate cut sits at ~68% (market pricing), but the data argues for caution. **High-duration growth stocks like TSLA benefit from rate-cut expectations** but are vulnerable if expectations get walked back.

### B. Geopolitics – U.S.-Iran Ceasefire Breakthrough
- A **60-day preliminary ceasefire extension** was announced on May 29, easing fears of a Strait of Hormuz blockade.
- **Crude oil prices dropped sharply**, providing a tailwind for equities (S&P 500 hit record highs).
- **Impact on TSLA:** Lower oil prices reduce the urgency for EV adoption in the short term, but the broader equity relief rally lifts all boats.

### C. Central Banks in Flux
- **Fed Leadership Transition:** Kevin Warsh takes over from Jerome Powell in June. Markets pricing in a potentially more hawkish/uncertain bias.
- **ECB:** French (2.8%) and Spanish (3.6%) inflation accelerating — increases probability of a June ECB rate hike, supporting EUR/USD.
- **Bank of Israel** cut rates to 3.75% amid domestic GDP contraction — highlighting divergence between war-impacted and non-war-impacted economies.

### D. China – Stagnation at the Margin
- **NBS Manufacturing PMI at 50.0** (stagnant); Non-Manufacturing PMI at 50.1 (barely expansionary).
- China's recovery remains fragile, bearish for commodities (copper, iron ore) and global trade sentiment.

### E. Market Structure
- **Cboe approved for extended options trading** (starting July 2026) on Magnificent 7 stocks — bullish for liquidity and price discovery.
- **SpaceX IPO valuation adjusted to $1.8T** (down from $2T) — slight cooling of "tech mania" sentiment.
